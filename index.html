About

01

I am a third-year PhD candidate in Economics at the Paris School of Economics and ENPC, supervised by Thierry Verdier, Carlos Winograd, and François Bourguignon. My dissertation, Essays on Intergenerational Mobility: New Evidence for Latin America, examines both the realities and the perceptions of economic mobility across the region.

Before starting my PhD, I worked as a research fellow at the Inter-American Development Bank's Research Department and held research and policy roles spanning Argentina's National Bank, the Buenos Aires City Government, and the OECD. I currently serve as Gender Coordinator at the World Inequality Lab.

I work across Spanish, English, and French, and use R, Stata, and LaTeX in my day-to-day research.
